List of Movies I Watched

My Neighbor Totoro

July 29 1989, running time 103 minutes. Directed and produced by Hayao Miyazaki, also produced by Toru Hara, music by Joe Hisaishi.

Satsuke and Mai move to a farm with their father while their mother is in the hospital. The nearby mysterious forest contains apparitions such as totoros, who take the sisters on a magical adventure.

Princess Mononoke

July 12 1997, running time 133 minutes. Directed by Hayao Miyazaki, produced by Toshio Suzuki, music by Joe Hisaishi.

On a mission to find the cure to a Tatarigami curse, Ashitaka finds himself in the middle of a war between the forest gods and Tatara, a mining colony. During his search he also meets San, the Mononoke Hime.

Spirited Away

July 20 2001, running time 125 minutes. Directed Hayao Miyazaki, produced by Toshio Suzuki, music by Joe Hisaishi.

After Chihiro's family takes a wrong turn on the way to their new home, they get lost in a forest and end up in a magical world where Chihiro's parents turn into pigs.

November 20 2004, running time 119 minutes. Directed by Hayao Miyazaki, produced by Toshio Suzuki, music by Joe Hisaishi.

A girl works in her parents' hat shop and sometimes meets the wizard Howl. When a witch sees her good fortune, she damns the girl to an early old age. The girl flees to Howl's moving castle.

Ponyo

July 19 2008, running time 101 minutes. Directed by Hayao Miyazaki, produced by Toshio Suzuki, music by Joe Hisaishi.

When five-year-old Sosuke is playing at the seaside, he finds a goldfish with a girl's face. She is the daughter of a wizard who renounced life on land for someone in the ocean. Sosuke is smitten and calls her Ponyo.

From Up on Poppy Hill

July 16 2011, running time 91 minutes. Directed by Goro Miyazaki, screenwriter(s) Hayao Miyazaki & Keiko Niwa, produced by Toshio Suzuki, music by Satoshi Takebe.

A group of teenagers from Yokohama try to save their school's clubhouse from the wrecking ball during preparations for the 1964 Olympic Games in rapidly modernizing Tokyo.
